CASE STUDY

Hi everyone. This is Taylor. I thought I'd give you some insight into the problem-solving process I use when I approach a project, by sharing with you how I developed this very site.

1. Ideate

In the Ideation phase I get to turn my imagination loose, exploring various possibilities for planning for the logo, color scheme, text and how the pages would be structured. The tools I used to refine my ideas included sketching, creating a designed resume, reference page, business card, and styled wireframes. I sketched some ideas for my personal idenity logo, then I had the difficult task of choosing one to bring to life with Adobe Illustrator. After I chose the perfect logo for me, I re-created and colorized my logo in Illustrator. I consistenly improved documents such as my resume, business card, etc. After finalization, I then created some stylized wireframes which helped me start creating the structure of my portfolio website with HTML/CSS.

2. Prototype

A prototype of my logo, resume, reference page, business card and my portfolio website was created so that I could have some memebers of my target audience try it out and give me their impressions.

3. Test

I prompted various target users to test the website. I had them narrate their intraction with it, which allowed me to identify items needing to be corrected in order for the app to better function.

Testing revealed that I should make the following improvements:

  • Use Better placement and spacing of text on About page's desktop and mobile

  • Improve text color to be more readable

  • Find better ways to showcase extra images without taking too much space, instead of just providing a link to an image/pdf.

Overview

The task I faced was to create a portfolio website to showcase Taylor's web design skills and let potential clients get into contact with me. I started with sketching a few ideas for my personal identity logo, then created and finalized a logo that best fit my personality and style. I then created a designed reference page, resume and business card where I then implemented my logo and color scheme. Once I finalized these documents, I then started to create a few different stylized wireframes for my portfolio website. After selecting a design I then created the structure of the portfolio webpage using HTML/CSS. I then implemented the same color scheme and design elements that was displayed on my logo, resume, business card, etc., and included screenshots of my past works.
